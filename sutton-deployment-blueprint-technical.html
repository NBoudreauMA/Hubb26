Executive Summary

This plain-language briefing describes how Sutton, MA will adopt the Polimorphic municipal platform with PublicLogic VAULT™ and ARCHIEVE™ safeguards while centering staff comfort, municipal ownership, and vendor accountability.

  • Outcomes: Unified resident services, statutory retention by design, evidentiary audit trails, and measurable improvements to permitting, licensing, and public works response times.
  • Scope: Resident portal (Polimorphic UI), SuttonCLERK licensing and meeting administration, SuttonFIX service desk, ERP/GIS integrations, VAULT™ retention enforcement, ARCHIEVE™ evidence chain, zero-trust infrastructure.
  • Timeline: Three-phase deployment (Pilot → Expand → Institutionalize) across a 12-month cadence anchored by quarterly governance reviews.
  • Cost Drivers: SaaS licensing, integrations (Munis, Cartegraph, MassGIS), AI governance staffing, and change management artifacts. Cost optimized through grant stacking and shared services participation.
  • Risk Posture: Compliance-first with legal holds, municipal data ownership, and vendor contracts tied to VAULT™/ARCHIEVE™ service-level evidence. Residual risk tracked in the program risk register.

System Architecture

PublicLogic’s architecture for Sutton is event-driven, compliance-first, and human-centered. Resident experiences are delivered through the Polimorphic front end while VAULT™ and ARCHIEVE™ enforce retention and auditability behind the scenes. Integrations with Massachusetts systems happen through secured adapters and a managed API gateway.

Architecture Narrative

Events move through four domains—Civic Services, Finance, Infrastructure, and Records. Each domain publishes to Confluent Kafka with schema-registry governance. Microservices operate within a Kubernetes-backed service mesh (Istio with mutual TLS) and apply policy-as-code modules to every transaction. VAULT™ attaches statutory retention metadata at ingestion, while ARCHIEVE™ provides notarized evidence snapshots for each workflow milestone.

AI and Automation Framework

We treat Sutton as the proving ground for progressive municipal AI adoption. PublicLogic’s practitioner guardrails ensure automation augments staff rather than replacing judgment.

  • Maturity Ladder: Assistive (AI suggestions logged for review) → Augmented (low-risk automation with human-on-the-loop) → Bounded Autonomy (automated actions with escalation, VAULT™ tags, and ARCHIEVE™ evidence).
  • Pipeline Architecture: Airflow DAGs orchestrate ETL, feature store (Feast) standardizes training data, and model monitoring dashboards track drift, fairness, and retention compliance.
  • Natural Language Understanding: Azure OpenAI models trained on municipal vocabulary operate inside a private network with policy-aware prompt templates and redaction guardrails.
  • Bounded Autonomy Safeguards: Multi-factor approvals, sandbox testing, and red-team simulations ensure compliance posture remains intact.

PublicLogic codifies Sutton’s core entities to support VAULT™ retention and ARCHIEVE™ audit evidence. Plain-language descriptions come first, followed by detailed tables and schemas.

Core Entity Summary

Requests, cases, residents, departments, tasks, service-level agreements, documents, and retention rules form the canonical data model. Each entity is tagged with policy identifiers so that data exports and legal holds stay consistent across systems.

Core Entity Catalogue
Entity Description Key Attributes Retention Anchor
Requests Resident-facing submissions (service issues, records requests, permit starts). Request ID, channel, resident ID, location, status, submitted timestamp. VAULT-RULE-SERVICE-02 — 3 years, pause during open holds.
Cases Internal work units managed by SuttonFIX and SuttonCLERK. Case number, owning department, SLA stage, assigned staff. VAULT-RULE-CASE-04 — 7 years or until litigation resolved.
Residents Constituent profiles with consent history and communication preferences. Resident GUID, preferred language, accessibility needs, consent flags. VAULT-RULE-RESIDENT-01 — Retain while active + 2 years.
Departments Organizational units tied to RACI and budgetary ownership. Department code, leadership contact, operating hours, grant tags. VAULT-RULE-GOV-03 — Permanent.
Tasks Actionable steps within a workflow (inspection, approval, communication). Task ID, SLA clock, status, evidence link, ARCHIEVE™ hash. Inherits parent case policy with sub-retention for attachments.
SLAs Response and resolution targets defined per service and channel. Service code, response hours, pause conditions, escalation matrix. VAULT-RULE-SLA-02 — 6 years.
Documents Files ingested from residents or generated internally. Document ID, classification, checksum, retention policy ID. Mapped via VAULT™ classification; destruction certificates stored.
Retention Rules Policy-as-code objects that drive VAULT™ actions. Policy ID, legal basis, trigger, duration, disposition method. Managed centrally with legal hold toggles.

Workflows (SOP-ready)

Each module includes clear triggers, decision points, SLA clocks, notifications, escalations, and audit evidence requirements. Staff can operationalize these SOPs immediately while vendors align automation to the same blueprint.

SuttonCLERK — Licensing and Board Administration

  • Trigger: Resident submits application or agenda item via civic portal or clerk intake desk.
  • Inputs: Completed form, supporting documents, fee payment reference, statutory citations.
  • Decision Points: Completeness review, board scheduling, vote outcome, appeal condition.
  • SLA: [SLA_HOURS=72] from submission to completeness determination with pauses for applicant action.
  • Notifications: Automated acknowledgement, board docket notice, decision email with retention summary.
  • Escalation: If awaiting board action beyond [BOARD_WAIT=14] calendar days, escalate to Town Administrator.
  • Audit Trail: ARCHIEVE™ stores meeting agenda reference, roll call vote, and disposition certificate.
workflow: suttonclerk-licensing.yaml 
workflow: suttonclerk-licensing
trigger: civic_portal_submission
sla:
  start: submission_timestamp
  target_hours: 72
  pause_conditions:
    - awaiting_applicant_documents
    - legal_hold_active
steps:
  - id: intake-review
    owner: Town Clerk
    actions:
      - validate_required_fields
      - request_missing_info
    notifications:
      - channel: email
        recipient: applicant
        template: clerk-intake-confirmation
  - id: board-scheduling
    owner: Clerk Operations
    conditions:
      - if license_type in ["alcohol", "entertainment"] then require_public_hearing: true
    outputs:
      - agenda_reference
      - meeting_date
  - id: decision-record
    owner: Board Chair
    completion:
      - capture_vote_result
      - attach_archieve_evidence
      - issue_decision_letter
holds:
  - type: legal_hold
    authority: Town Counsel
    effect: pause_disposition
retention_policy: VAULT-RULE-CLERK-01

Public Records Request (PRR) Handling

  • Trigger: Submission via SuttonCLERK portal or email to records@sutton-ma.gov.
  • Inputs: Request description, statutory timeline, requester contact, fee waiver eligibility.
  • Decision Points: Clarification required, exemption review, legal hold checks.
  • SLA: [PRR_INITIAL_RESPONSE=10] business days pursuant to M.G.L. c.66 §10, with trackable extensions.
  • Notifications: Acknowledgement letter, extension notice, production delivery with retention summary.
  • Escalation: Missed deadlines escalate to Records Access Officer and Town Counsel.
  • Audit Trail: ARCHIEVE™ captures each correspondence, exemption citation, and final delivery manifest.
