Understanding Hubbardston's Budget Process

Welcome! This guide will help you understand how the town budget works, why it matters, and how you can be part of shaping Hubbardston’s future. The budget is more than numbers—it’s a roadmap for our town’s priorities, services, and future planning.

What is the Budget?

The town budget is the annual financial plan that allocates resources to schools, public safety, infrastructure, and more. It reflects Hubbardston’s values and long-term planning.

How the Budget is Created

The budget process follows a structured timeline:

  1. Department Requests (Fall): Each town department submits a funding request based on needs and priorities.
  2. Town Administrator Review (Winter): The Town Administrator evaluates requests and drafts a proposed budget.
  3. Finance Committee & Select Board Review (Spring): These boards analyze the budget, make adjustments, and prepare for Town Meeting.
  4. Town Meeting Approval (June): Residents vote on the final budget, determining funding for services and projects.

Key Budget Documents

  • Revenue Summary: Explains where Hubbardston gets its funding (taxes, state aid, fees).
  • Expenditures Plan: Shows how funds are allocated to different services and projects.
  • Capital Improvement Plan: Details major long-term investments like road repairs and facility upgrades.
  • Financial Policies: Provides guidelines for responsible spending and savings.

Why This Matters

The budget affects everything in town, from school programs to road repairs. Your voice matters! Being informed means you can help prioritize what’s most important.

How to Get Involved

  • Attend Public Meetings: Share your thoughts on funding priorities.
  • Review Budget Documents: Access reports and summaries online.
  • Provide Feedback: Submit questions or concerns to town officials.
  • Vote at Town Meeting: Have a direct say in the final budget decisions.
