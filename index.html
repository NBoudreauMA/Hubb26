FY26 Budget Summary

Welcome & Fiscal Year 2026 Budget Summary

Welcome to Hubbardston’s Fiscal Year 2026 Budget Portal—a space dedicated to transparency, smart financial planning, and ensuring that every dollar spent aligns with our town’s long-term vision.

This year’s budget reflects our ongoing commitment to stability, strategic investments, and responsible financial management. With a total budget of $11,851,233—a 6.32% increase over FY25—we’re ensuring that critical services, infrastructure, and public safety remain priorities. At the same time, projected revenues of $11,719,755 represent a 5.75% growth, reinforcing Hubbardston’s financial health while maintaining our commitment to prudent spending.

Key investments in infrastructure, public safety, and modernization continue to drive our fiscal strategy. This budget also strengthens our financial position by responsibly using $131,478 in free cash, targeting one-time expenses that improve services while reducing future financial strain.

What’s new? We’re taking a modernized approach to budget engagement. This new web-based portal provides real-time access to financial data, spending priorities, and insights into how Hubbardston is planning for a sustainable future. Our commitment remains clear: ensuring long-term financial health while delivering high-quality services that enhance our community.

Thank you for taking the time to engage with this process—we encourage residents to explore the budget, provide feedback, and be part of shaping Hubbardston’s future.

Respectfully submitted,

Public Works & Infrastructure

  • The Department of Public Works (DPW) budget includes necessary increases for road maintenance, winter operations, and equipment investments.
  • Addressing long-term infrastructure needs while maintaining cost efficiency.
  • Equipment investments for long-term efficiency and proactive infrastructure management.

Planning & Land Use

  • The part-time Town Planner position has already proven to be a major asset, helping to secure grants, improve permitting processes, and guide land-use planning efforts.
  • The town has already benefited from increased grant applications and stronger regulatory processes.
  • Strategic land-use planning leading to long-term financial benefits and more organized growth.

Modernizing Town Operations

  • Investments in the Administrative Services Coordinator and Executive Assistant positions have enhanced efficiency, expanded public access, and increased responsiveness to constituent needs.
  • The town is actively revising fee schedules to align with regional input, ensuring that fees remain fair, transparent, and structured to support municipal operations.
  • Investments in Hubb Connect, e-permitting, and workflow automation will lead to a more streamlined, responsive, and tech-forward municipal government.

Strategic Use of Free Cash

This year, Hubbardston has further reduced its reliance on free cash, ensuring that one-time expenses critical to operations, infrastructure, and resident services are fully funded while maintaining fiscal responsibility. These funds are being applied strategically to maintain service levels, sustain necessary investments, and support town operations, reducing the likelihood of service disruptions or deferred maintenance that could create larger costs in future budgets.

The $123,007 in free cash supports a combination of personnel investments, infrastructure improvements, contractual obligations, and service enhancements, allowing the town to continue operating efficiently without impacting core services.

Key Considerations & Fiscal Oversight

  • Education costs remain the largest financial risk. The Quabbin assessment may exceed current estimates, requiring potential mid-year adjustments.
  • Revenue tracking is essential. Local receipts, particularly excise taxes and public safety revenue, should be closely monitored to ensure projections remain on track.
  • Free cash use has been reduced, and the town will continue working toward minimizing reliance on these funds for balancing budgets in future years.
  • The budget is balanced, but flexibility remains tight. While revenues are growing, expenses—particularly education and municipal obligations—continue to outpace revenue growth. Maintaining a disciplined approach to financial management will be key to ensuring fiscal stability in the coming years.

The FY26 budget prioritizes responsible fiscal management, service continuity, and long-term sustainability. While we continue to navigate rising costs, Hubbardston remains in a stronger financial position than many neighboring communities, thanks to careful planning, strategic investments, and efficiency gains from initiatives like Hubb Connect and workflow automation.

With continued oversight, expanded resident services, and proactive financial management, Hubbardston is positioned to thrive.
