System Architecture

The platform is delivered as an event-driven mesh where the Polimorphic resident portal feeds structured requests into a workflow layer spanning [CLERK_MODULE_NAME], [SERVICE_DESK_NAME], and [PERMIT_MODULE_NAME]. VAULT™ outcomes apply policy IDs, legal hold flags, and retention timelines; ARCHIEVE™ outcomes generate immutable evidence packages. Adapter services connect to [ERP_NAME] (Confirm with municipality), [GIS_NAME] (Confirm with municipality), payments, and Microsoft 365 while audit/reporting services expose filtered exports without duplicating source-of-truth records.

Diagram (textual): Resident Portal → Workflow Orchestrator → VAULT™ Outcome Layer → ARCHIEVE™ Evidence Layer → Integration Adapters (ERP/GIS/Payments/O365/Audit) → Reporting & Exports.

Security, Privacy & Retention

  • Role-Based Access: Least privilege roles spanning Clerk, DPW, Inspectional Services, Finance, Vendor Support, each mapped to specific workflows and export capabilities.
  • Encryption: TLS 1.2+ for all transport channels; AES-256-backed storage with secret rotation aligned to 201 CMR 17.00 guidance.
  • Logging & Redaction: Immutable audit logs with redact-once workflows for sensitive data, maintaining archieveEvidenceId continuity.
  • Legal Holds: Authorized by Town Counsel; banners applied; retention clock paused until dual acknowledgement release recorded.

Disaster Recovery Note

Quarterly continuity exercises validate backup restores, failover of workflow services, and re-application of retention policies. Evidence packages remain immutable and are cross-verified via checksum before resuming operations.

Change-Log Convention

All configuration changes recorded in a shared changelog with timestamps, responsible owner, policy references, and associated archieveEvidenceId for traceability.
